# CRM Automation Playbook

## Purpose

Automation should reduce manual work, improve consistency, and increase visibility.

The goal is not more automation.

The goal is better execution.

---

## Lead Management Automation

### Lead Assignment

Trigger:
- New lead created

Action:
- Assign owner based on territory
- Assign owner based on industry
- Assign owner based on company size

---

## Lead Qualification Automation

Trigger:
- Lead score threshold reached

Action:
- Update lifecycle stage
- Notify sales representative
- Create follow-up task

---

## Meeting Booking Automation

Trigger:
- Meeting booked

Action:
- Create opportunity
- Update lifecycle stage
- Notify account owner

---

## Opportunity Management Automation

Trigger:
- Opportunity created

Action:
- Generate onboarding tasks
- Create activity reminders
- Assign stakeholders

---

## Follow-Up Automation

Trigger:
- No activity for 7 days

Action:
- Notify opportunity owner
- Create follow-up task

---

## Lost Opportunity Automation

Trigger:
- Opportunity marked lost

Action:
- Capture lost reason
- Add competitor information
- Enroll into nurture workflow

---

## Customer Handoff Automation

Trigger:
- Opportunity marked won

Action:
- Create onboarding project
- Notify customer success team
- Schedule kickoff meeting

---

## Data Hygiene Automation

Trigger:
- Missing required field

Action:
- Alert record owner
- Create remediation task

---

## Automation Design Principles

1. Automate repetitive tasks.
2. Never automate bad processes.
3. Prioritize visibility.
4. Review workflows quarterly.
5. Measure workflow effectiveness.

---

## Key Principle

Automation should remove friction.

Not create complexity.
