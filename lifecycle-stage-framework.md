# Lifecycle Stage Framework

## Purpose

Lifecycle stages help track a prospect's journey from first touch to customer.

A well-designed lifecycle framework improves visibility, reporting, automation, and revenue forecasting.

---

## Stage 1: Subscriber

### Definition
A contact who has engaged with content but has not shown buying intent.

### Examples
- Newsletter signup
- Webinar attendee
- Content download

### Goal
Increase engagement and qualification.

---

## Stage 2: Lead

### Definition
A contact that matches the Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

### Qualification Criteria
- Relevant industry
- Relevant company size
- Valid contact information

### Goal
Determine sales readiness.

---

## Stage 3: Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL)

### Definition
A lead showing meaningful buying signals.

### Examples
- Multiple website visits
- Demo request
- Pricing page visits

### Goal
Prepare for sales engagement.

---

## Stage 4: Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)

### Definition
A lead validated by the sales team.

### Qualification Questions
- Is there a business problem?
- Is there urgency?
- Is there budget?
- Is there authority?

### Goal
Create an opportunity.

---

## Stage 5: Opportunity

### Definition
An active sales conversation with revenue potential.

### Requirements
- Discovery completed
- Need confirmed
- Value identified

### Goal
Progress toward proposal and close.

---

## Stage 6: Customer

### Definition
A signed and paying customer.

### Goal
Deliver value and ensure adoption.

---

## Stage 7: Expansion

### Definition
An existing customer with upsell or cross-sell potential.

### Goal
Increase customer lifetime value.

---

## Stage 8: Advocate

### Definition
A customer actively promoting the business.

### Examples
- Referrals
- Testimonials
- Case studies

### Goal
Create growth through advocacy.

---

## Design Principles

1. Every stage must have clear entry criteria.
2. Every stage must have clear exit criteria.
3. Lifecycle stages must support reporting.
4. Automation should align with lifecycle progression.
5. Simplicity beats complexity.

---

## Key Principle

A lifecycle stage should represent a change in customer reality.

Not a change in internal activity.
