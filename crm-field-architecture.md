# CRM Field Architecture

## Purpose

A CRM is only as good as the data it captures.

Field architecture should support reporting, automation, forecasting, and decision-making.

---

## Contact Fields

### Required Fields

- First Name
- Last Name
- Email
- Phone Number
- Job Title
- Company Name

### Recommended Fields

- LinkedIn URL
- Location
- Industry
- Lead Source

---

## Company Fields

### Required Fields

- Company Name
- Industry
- Website
- Company Size

### Recommended Fields

- Annual Revenue
- Technology Stack
- Growth Stage
- Headquarters Location

---

## Opportunity Fields

### Required Fields

- Opportunity Name
- Deal Value
- Pipeline Stage
- Expected Close Date
- Opportunity Owner

### Recommended Fields

- Lead Source
- Competitor
- Use Case
- Risk Level

---

## Lead Qualification Fields

### Fit Criteria

- Industry Match
- Company Size Match
- ICP Score

### Intent Criteria

- Website Activity
- Content Engagement
- Demo Request

---

## Reporting Fields

Track:

- Lead Source
- Campaign Source
- Lifecycle Stage
- Opportunity Stage
- Revenue Attribution

---

## Automation Fields

Used for:

- Lead Routing
- Workflow Triggers
- Notifications
- Follow-Up Tasks

---

## Design Principles

1. Every field must have a purpose.
2. Avoid duplicate data.
3. Keep required fields minimal.
4. Design for reporting first.
5. Design for automation second.

---

## Key Principle

Bad CRM data creates bad decisions.

Good field architecture creates reliable insights.
